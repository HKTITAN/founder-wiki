# Better, Faster, or Cheaper

[[Garry Tan]] argues that being first to market is overrated. Facebook was the 20th social network. Snapchat was not the first messaging app. Discord was not the first chat app. "You don't have to be first, but you do need to be different" [1]. Every startup must be better, faster, or cheaper than existing alternatives. "If you don't have that, you can't succeed, your startup will die" [1].

## Being First Can Be a Disadvantage

Before Instacart, there was Webvan. Before Facebook, there was Friendster and Six Degrees. Tan uses his own experience: Posterous launched five years after WordPress but still grew fast because it was better in one specific way -- post by email [1].

Investors do favor novelty, which creates a bias founders must navigate. Brand new business models are usually driven by new technologies, new online behaviors, new demographics, new capabilities, or new regulation. But not every startup can find a blue ocean. "Sometimes you have to wade into a red ocean: a space with lots of competitors" [1].

## Better

Being better means providing a clearly superior experience. Tan identifies several patterns [1]:

- **Technical excellence**: Google was better than AltaVista through PageRank. Dropbox was better than a USB drive through seamless syncing.
- **Underserved audience**: Discord focused on gamers (low CPU requirements so games could still run). Airbnb targeted conference goers when no hotels were available. [[Coinbase: From Bitcoin at Five Dollars to a Hundred Billion Dollar Company|Coinbase]] served early crypto believers when the only alternative was the sketchy Mt. Gox.

## Faster

Being faster means reducing time for the customer. Tan's examples [1]:

- **Software replacing paper**: [[Flexport: From Solo Founder to Eight Billion Dollar Logistics Platform|Flexport]] speeds air freight by two to three days and resolves insurance claims six times faster by replacing paper-and-pencil processes with software. "It's easy to be faster than your competitors when your competitors are literally using paper and pencil."
- **Supply liquidity**: Uber used software to scale the world's largest mobile workforce. More drivers meant faster response times.
- **Technical performance**: Facebook stayed fast while Friendster choked on unoptimized MySQL and Java Server Pages. "Speed matters a lot, especially for consumer scenarios."

## Cheaper

Being cheaper means delivering the same or better value at lower cost. Tan's examples [1]:

- **SaaS replacing infrastructure**: Shopify charged $29/month versus running your own Magento server, enabling "a new generation of brand and store that just couldn't have existed without them."
- **New business model**: Lyft applied Uber's model but with citizen drivers instead of black cars, making rides far cheaper. Instacart did asset-light grocery delivery, avoiding Webvan's warehouse and fleet costs.
- **Margin elimination**: Amazon's mantra -- "their margin is our opportunity" -- cut retail margins by removing physical stores. Robinhood brought trading fees to zero by selling order flow data.

## Combining the Framework

These three axes are not mutually exclusive. Many successful startups combine two or three. Flexport is both faster (software vs. paper) and cheaper (efficiency gains). The key diagnostic question: "Is it better, faster, or cheaper than alternatives?" If the answer is no on all three, "you should find a way to become that, or you need to find a new idea" [1].
