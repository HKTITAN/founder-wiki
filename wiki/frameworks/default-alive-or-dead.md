# Default Alive or Default Dead

A diagnostic framework for startup survival introduced by Paul Graham. The question: assuming expenses stay constant and revenue grows at its current trend, will the startup reach profitability on the money it has? ([Default alive or default dead](https://www.ycombinator.com/library/95-default-alive-or-default-dead))

## The Framework

**Default alive:** Revenue [[Growth|growth]] will cover expenses before money runs out. The company survives even if it never raises again.

**Default dead:** Expenses exceed what revenue growth can cover in the available time. The company must either raise more money (see [[Fundraising]]) or cut costs.

Graham's alarming claim: half the founders he talks to do not know which category they are in ([Default alive or default dead](https://www.ycombinator.com/library/95-default-alive-or-default-dead)). This ignorance is a core [[Financial Survival]] failure mode.

## The Fatal Pinch

The worst state: default dead, with slow [[Growth|growth]], and not enough time or money to fix either. By the time founders recognize a fatal pinch, options have narrowed to near-zero ([Default alive or default dead](https://www.ycombinator.com/library/95-default-alive-or-default-dead)).

## Plan A / Plan B

Graham recommends maintaining two plans:

**Plan A:** The growth plan — [[Hiring|hire]], spend, scale. This is the default mode when things are going well.

**Plan B:** The survival plan — cut to a skeleton crew, reach ramen profitability. This plan should be written down in advance, with a specific date by which the founder switches to it if Plan A is not working ([Default alive or default dead](https://www.ycombinator.com/library/95-default-alive-or-default-dead)).

Having Plan B written down is what distinguishes careful founders from reckless ones. The plan itself may never be executed, but the act of writing it forces the founder to understand their real financial position. This ties directly to the [[Founder Mindset]] of operating with clear-eyed realism about [[Financial Survival]].
