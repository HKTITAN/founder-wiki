# Board Governance

## Board Meetings

Hariharan argues that the biggest mistake is treating board meetings as status updates. Best CEOs spend only the first 45 minutes on KPIs and allocate the majority of time to 1-2 strategic deep-dives on the hardest problems ([How to create and manage a board](https://www.ycombinator.com/library/3w-how-to-create-and-manage-a-board)).

Written memos (pioneered by companies like Segment and Faire) are superior to slides for strategic board discussion. They force clearer thinking and allow board members to engage with nuance rather than reacting to bullet points ([How to create and manage a board](https://www.ycombinator.com/library/3w-how-to-create-and-manage-a-board)).

## Building Board Relationships

The #1 attribute needed in a board member is trust, and the only way to build it is time. Hariharan recommends getting to know VC partners 6-9 months before [[Fundraising|fundraising]] begins ([How to create and manage a board](https://www.ycombinator.com/library/3w-how-to-create-and-manage-a-board)).

Monthly 2-page update emails accomplish three things: board members can help with specific asks, quarterly meetings are better-prepared, and the CEO gains a forcing function for reflection. This practice ties into broader [[Management|management]] discipline.

## Board Control

Kwon and Harris warn that the most significant way founders lose control at Series A is the 2-2-1 board structure (2 founders, 2 investors, 1 independent) — because it means founders can be fired from their own company. A 2-1 structure preserves founder control ([A standard and clean Series A term sheet](https://www.ycombinator.com/library/4P-a-standard-and-clean-series-a-term-sheet)).

"When an investor says that they're committed to partnering with you for the long-term but then tells you something else with the terms they insist on, believe the terms." Series A documents set precedent for future rounds — bad terms compound and are difficult to unwind ([A standard and clean Series A term sheet](https://www.ycombinator.com/library/4P-a-standard-and-clean-series-a-term-sheet)). This connects directly to [[Financial Survival]] and long-term [[Fundraising]] strategy.
