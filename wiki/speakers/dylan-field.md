# Dylan Field

Dylan Field is the co-founder of Figma, the collaborative design tool that Adobe acquired for $20 billion [1]. He dropped out of Brown University to start the company. [[Garry Tan]] profiles his journey as a canonical example of a "10-year overnight success" -- the compound effect of navigating thousands of small decisions over a decade [1].

## Worldview

**Explore new technology, but walk toward a real problem.** Field and his co-founder Evan Wallace started in "exploration mode" in 2012, knowing they wanted to do something with WebGL (a new way to use the GPU in the browser) and creative tools. They cared about accessibility: "We agreed with a statement that Bret Victor made, which is that if you can't use a tool because it's too complex, it's a moral wrong" [1]. The danger of technology-first exploration is building something meaningless -- they briefly worked on a meme generator before Field looked in the mirror and said, "I dropped out of Brown for this?" [1].

**Management is a skill, not a personality trait.** Field describes management as "a very different skill from leadership. They're related, but they're different. And management is a skill that you have to build just like any other skill" [1]. A year into Figma, he was struggling with it: he made hires that were not the right cultural fit, he micromanaged, and he was not empowering people enough. "When you hire smart people, you got to give them a way to really contribute and to blow you away and to let them do things that you would never think of" [1].

The crisis peaked when employees confronted him directly: "People were really unhappy with me. And it was a moment where, you know, they're like, 'Okay, we really need you to get help'" [1]. This happened while Figma still had not launched and while Field's father was dying of cancer. The resolution came from hiring the company's first outside manager, from whom Field learned management skills. Tan frames this as the universal challenge for technical founders who want to remain CEO: "Every highly talented technical founder who starts and wants to stay in that CEO position for the long haul will end up having to learn what Dylan learned" [1].
