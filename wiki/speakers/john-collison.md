# John Collison

John Collison is the co-founder and President of Stripe. He and his brother [[Patrick Collison]] started the company while both were in college. John focuses on operations, engineering, and growth at Stripe.

## Worldview

**Interview in ways that work for you, not what convention dictates.** John Collison argues that founders should not cargo-cult standard interview processes. When Stripe hired its first engineer, the Collisons flew the candidate out and spent a weekend coding together -- "the only way we could really tell." For business development hires, they asked candidates to analyze an existing Stripe partnership and propose improvements. "Even though it's not my domain area, I am actually confident enough that I can judge those pretty well" [1].

**Push people off the cliff.** Collison advocates for immediate immersion over gradual onboarding. Engineers should be committing code on their first day. Business hires should be in real meetings on their first day. "Sometimes there's a tendency to be tentative and help ease people in. We're much more of the 'push people off the cliff' school" [1].

**Give people a chance to lead, but make it reversible.** Some early Stripe employees successfully transitioned from individual contributors to managing large teams. Others tried management and decided "I'm really glad I tried it because I never want to do that again." The company ensures both paths -- management and individual contribution -- offer equal impact. "My strong preference is that you give as many people a shot as possible" [1].

## Distinct Perspective

While Patrick Collison tends toward strategic and philosophical framings (culture as bandwidth solution, time horizons shifting), John Collison is more tactical and operationally direct. His contributions center on how things actually work in practice: the specific mechanics of interviewing, onboarding, and developing people. His advice to give rapid cultural feedback to new hires -- "everything from high level how you're doing at your job to minor cultural pointers" -- reflects an operator's focus on the daily interactions that shape culture [1].

He is also candid about the difficulty of early recruiting. Stripe's second employee almost did not join because his best friend staged "a full-on assault" the night before he was supposed to start. That friend later joined Stripe himself [1].
